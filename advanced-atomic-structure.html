Quantum numbers

Electrons in an atom can be distinguished or characterized using quantum numbers.Quantum numbers are a set of numbers used to describe the position of an electron in an atom and the energy they possess.

The four quantum numbers are:

  1. Principle quantum number- Describes the energy level that an electron occupies
  2. Azimuthal quantum number- Describes the electron subshell.
  3. Magnetic quantum number- Represents the specific orbital within the subshell.
  4. Spin quantum number-Describes the spin of the electron.

How electrons fill orbitals in the atom

They fill orbitals adhering to the following principles and rule.

  • Aufbau principle- States that electrons fill the atomic orbitals of the lowest energy first before filling the orbitals occupying higher energy levels
  • Pauli exclusion principle- States that each orbital is allowed a maximum of two electrons which should have opposite spin
  • Hund's rule- States that, in a case where orbital are of equal energy, electrons will only start to pair up once allowed the degenerate orbitals are half-filled

Electron shells are basically the energy levels and are represented by the principle quantum number e.g 1, 2, 3, 4.
